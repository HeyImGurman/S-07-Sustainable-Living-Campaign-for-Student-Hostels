# S-07: Sustainable Living Campaign for Student Hostels

## Social Challenge
**University hostels consume significant energy, water, and single-use plastics with minimal awareness or incentive for sustainable behavior among residents. Individual actions at scale have meaningful environmental impact.**

## Background
Living communities inside campuses present a unique opportunity to build environmental responsibility. Nudge theories and structured competitions can dramatically reduce resource waste.

## Project Objectives
1. Measure baseline energy, water, and waste generation in 2-3 hostel blocks
2. Design a behavioral nudge campaign to reduce consumption
3. Implement a 'Green Hostel' competition between blocks
4. Train hostel residents as sustainability ambassadors
5. Propose infrastructure interventions to hostel management (LED retrofit, sensor taps)
6. Document measurable reduction in resource consumption over the campaign period

## Stakeholders
| Stakeholder | Role |
| :--- | :--- |
| Hostel residents | Primary participants |

| University sustainability cell (if exists) | Institutional partner |
| Local electricity/water utility | Data validation |
| Student union | Campaign co-organizer |

## Requirements
- **Research**: Study behavioral nudge theory (Thaler & Sunstein); review energy benchmarks for comparable university hostels; identify no-cost and low-cost interventions.
- **Survey**: Energy/water usage habits; sustainability attitude scale; post-campaign attitude + behavior change.
- **Execution**: Baseline data collection; campaign design and rollout; Green Hostel competition; ambassador training; infrastructure proposal.
- **Documentation**: Baseline data, campaign materials, competition results, ambassador training records, utility data (if accessible).
